Project 2: Fictional Objects

In Project 2, I evaluated a chosen object’s and redesigned it, creating a prototype that made user experience more meaningful and useful.
Tools used: Analog

Brainstorming

Original Object: Marie Antoinette-themed jewelry holder

  • This jewelry holder was purchased in a gift shop at the Louvre Museum in Paris, France.
  • Why it's meaningful to me and other users:
    • Feelings of sentiment and memories
    • Ease of use and efficiency
      •

Research

To learn more about souvenirs and emotional attachment to objects, I researched existing souvenirs and read articles about how an object's context, design, and composition further enhance a person's experience with it.
I visited "What are Souvenirs and why do we buy them?" to understand the meaning behind souvenirs.
Then, I read "Object attachment as we grow older" to learn about emotional attachment to objects.

Planning

How Might I... Question:
How might I increase usability in the jewelry holder so that I can make it more effective and compact for the user while also improving certain features like form, reliability, and sound?

Sketches

Initial Prototype

Materials: Bristol paper, tape, superglue

- Who: Jewelry-wearers and tourists.
- What: A more improved version of the same object. Features more sections in the holder, padded bottoms and a separator between the mirror and sectioned-off jewelry, and a stand for the holder to sit upright.
- Where: Desks or vanities for users (bought in the Louvre).
- How: Utilizing more sections, layers of padding and separators, extending stand.
- Why: Improves usability and convenience for the user, as well as enhances certain features including use of the mirror and more compactness.

Prototype Transition

Switching Focus:
The current prototype improves usability and convenience, but not much else.
- I dialed it back down and went back to the basics – why did I find the object meaningful to me? And I found myself thinking about my memories studying abroad, the friends I made along the way, etc.
- I brainstormed more ways to incorporate increased emotional connections to the object itself, as shown in the below diagram.
Adding this second layer of both meaning and literal storage within the object would allow users to utilize it in more versatile ways and contexts, not just as a jewelry holder that sits in their room all day.

In addition, I decided to improve on the medium used for the prototype, because aesthetics played a large role in appeal and meaning for the original object. It wasn’t just another holder purchased on Amazon; it was a Parisian-themed one purchased on a trip.
Using sturdier materials would also increase quality + presentation, and help with demonstrating its usefulness.

Final Prototype

Materials: Wood, felt, chains, metal sheet, keychain, paper, bristol paper, superglue

- Who: Jewelry-wearers and tourists.
- What: A memento box combined as a jewelry holder & keychain.
- Where: High portability + compactness + keychain -> used on trips, attached to bags, backpacks, etc.
- How: Added keychain, kept additional jewelry section, more literal padding on the jewelry, additional box on bottom for storage and compatibility as a memory box.
- Why: Allows users to personalize the object and make it more meaningful and sentimental to them through its ability to be a holder/box of memories and connections to people and experiences through photos and other small items.

Other Considerations

- Constraints: Constraints were a bit harder to explicitly implement in the physical prototype because of the material, but some included how far the top lid could be opened, how tightly the box could close, etc.
- Affordances: Claspability through the keychain, “dangling”, and opening and closing (because of the box-like nature of the object).
- Signifiers: Main one was the small handle on the keychain. Other explicit signifiers could compromise the aesthetics of the object.
